Skanda Shreekar Sira Prabhakar acts in a play

Ghosts Act 2 by Henrik Ibsen

MANDERS. What do you mean by that? MRS. ALVING. [Drumming on the window frame.] I ought never to have concealed the facts of Alving's life. But at that time I dared not do anything else--I was afraid, partly on my own account. I was such a coward. MANDERS. A coward? MRS. ALVING. If people had come to know anything, they would have said--"Poor man! with a runaway wife, no wonder he kicks over the traces." MANDERS. Such remarks might have been made with a certain show of right. MRS. ALVING. [Looking steadily at him.] If I were what I ought to be, I should go to Oswald and say, "Listen, my boy: your father led a vicious life--" MANDERS. Merciful heavens--!
